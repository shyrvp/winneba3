The Future of Winneba Sacred Lagoon

When traditional culture faces common global challenges

Description

The Effutu culture in Winneba, Ghana is founded on a rich tradition of land and water gods, some of whom were accompanying the founding tribe as it made its way south. Other gods have been enshrined at specific sites in and around Winneba. Taboos of everyday life were also established and while diminishing in power, continue to persist today in the everyday lives of the community. Interestingly, these ancient taboos reinforce the conservation approaches to the management of natural waterways and reinforce the mission of the modern Ramsar designation. However, urban growth as well as other environmental problems have been threatening this big sacred site in recent years.
